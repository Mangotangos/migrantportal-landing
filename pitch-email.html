Church & Nonprofit Pitch Emails

A 3-email sequence to invite churches and nonprofit organizations to join MigrantPortal as implementing partners. Personalize the [bracketed fields] before sending.

How to Use This Sequence

  • Send Email 1 cold — no prior relationship needed. Lead with the story.
  • Wait 5–7 days. If no reply, send Email 2 — add social proof and a concrete ask.
  • Wait 5 days. If still no reply, send Email 3 — short, direct close. This is your last touch.
  • Personalize each email: use the pastor/director's name, the church name, and their city.
  • Best send times: Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10am or 1–3pm local time.

The Story

Send on Day 1 — cold outreach, no prior contact needed
Subject Line
A family arrived in [City] with nothing. A church responded in 3 hours.
Email Body
Hi [Pastor/Director Name], I wanted to share something that happened on our platform last month. A mother and her two kids arrived in [City] — no food, no contacts, no idea where to turn. She found MigrantPortal and submitted a need. Three hours later, a local church showed up with groceries, a warm coat for her daughter, and a phone number to call if she needed anything else. That's MigrantPortal in a sentence: we connect migrant families in crisis with churches and nonprofits who are ready to help — right in their own city. We're building our partner network in [City] and I'd love to see [Church/Org Name] be part of it. Becoming a partner is free and takes about 15 minutes to set up. Your team gets a simple dashboard to see needs in your area, respond to what you can, and track the impact your community is making. Would it be okay if I sent you a short overview? Or if you'd like to explore right now, you can see real needs in your area at migrantportal.com. Where help meets hope — I hope [Church/Org Name] will be part of that. Nirmal Samuel Founder, MigrantPortal Foundation hello@migrantportal.com migrantportal.com
Personalization tip Replace [City] with the city where this church is located. Replace [Church/Org Name] with their actual name. If you know of a real need in their city from the platform, mention it (without identifying details) for maximum relevance.

The Impact

Send on Day 6–8 if no reply to Email 1
Subject Line
120 families helped. 30 churches. Here's what partners actually do.
Email Body
Hi [Pastor/Director Name], Following up on my last note about MigrantPortal. I thought it might help to share what partner churches actually do on our platform — it's less work than you might think. Here's what's happening across our network right now: • 120+ migrant families have received help • 340+ community volunteers are actively responding to needs • 30+ churches and nonprofits are coordinating response • Needs range from food and clothing to translation, legal referrals, and temporary housing As a partner organization, your team gets: → A free dashboard showing needs in [City] you can respond to → Ability to post your own volunteer opportunities → Monthly impact reports showing what your community accomplished → Connection to a growing network of like-minded organizations Most of our partner churches spend 1–2 hours per week reviewing and responding to needs. No new staff. No new programs. Just a new way to activate the compassion your congregation already has. Would a 20-minute call work this week or next? I'd love to show you how it works. Nirmal Samuel Founder, MigrantPortal Foundation hello@migrantportal.com migrantportal.com
Personalization tip If you have a specific stat for their city (e.g., "We have 12 open needs in Kansas City right now"), add it after the bullet list. It dramatically increases response rates.

The Close

Send on Day 12–14 — final follow-up, keep it short
Subject Line
Last note — is MigrantPortal a fit for [Church/Org Name]?
Email Body
Hi [Pastor/Director Name], I don't want to keep showing up in your inbox if this isn't a fit — so I'll make this my last note. MigrantPortal connects migrant families in [City] with churches and nonprofits ready to help. It's free, it takes 15 minutes to set up, and your team keeps full control of what you respond to. One question: is serving migrant families something [Church/Org Name] is currently doing or exploring? If yes — I'd love to connect. If not, no worries at all. Either way, you can see how it works at migrantportal.com/how-migrantportal-works.html. Thank you for your time. Nirmal Samuel Founder, MigrantPortal Foundation hello@migrantportal.com
Why this works The "last note" framing removes pressure and triggers a guilt-response in the recipient. The single yes/no question is easy to answer. Short emails on the final touch consistently outperform longer ones.
